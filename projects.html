Thesis: Overcrowded Housing of Chinese Immigrants

My master's thesis in City and Regional Planning focused on overcrowded housing of Chinese Immigrants in Manhattan's Chinatown and Sunset Park. The goal was to determine the appropriateness of smaller unit design to meet the problems of overcrowding among Chinese immigrants.

The following objectives were achieved through the use of census data, literature review, interviews, questionnaires, and case studies:

  1. Provide background on Chinese immigrants in NYC (Chinatown and Sunset Park)
  2. Research & evaluate existing living conditions of residents
  3. Research the needs and desires of current residents
  4. Research and analyze appropriateness and affordability of microhousing
  5. Research other appropriate housing typologies

Brooklyn CB16: Vacant Lots Project

The goal of this project was to provide Brooklyn Community Board 16 (Ocean Hill and Brownsville) with an updated list of vacant lots and buildings within the District. This list will be used to inform the Land Use Committee about potential developments and projects available within the District.

The process to reach the final deliverables was:

  1. Merge existing databases of vacant lots from NYC Pluto (tax lot database) and 596 Acres.
  2. Walk through Community District 16 to verify the vacant lots listed in existing databases and to add any lots or spaces not listed.
  3. Find the zoning type, property owner, and available Floor Area Ratio to the vacant spaces.

From the beginning of February through mid-April, I walked through Brownsville and Ocean Hill twice a week, jotting down and taking pictures of properties that were vacant, under development, or marked as vacant but being used for parking.
